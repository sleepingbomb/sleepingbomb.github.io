Project Overview

Our final robot implementation brings together months of research, simulation, and iterative design into a functional biomimetic jumping mechanism.

The design features a four-bar linkage that replicates the mechanical advantage and motion characteristics of a grasshopper's hind leg. Energy is stored in cardstock spring elements during the compression phase, then released explosively to achieve the jump.

Actuation is provided by SG90 micro servo motors controlled via an ESP32 microcontroller, enabling precise timing and repeatable jump cycles. The entire mechanism is fabricated using laser-cut laminates, demonstrating the power of foldable robotics principles.
