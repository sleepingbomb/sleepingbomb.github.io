Design Optimization

Data-driven parameter optimization using simulation-based analysis to maximize jumping performance while respecting manufacturing and actuation constraints.

Overview

This section presents our design optimization workflow for the grasshopper-inspired jumping robot. Using a data-driven dynamics approach, we systematically explored the design space to identify optimal parameters for maximum jump height and forward displacement.

The optimization process leverages MuJoCo physics simulations to evaluate thousands of parameter combinations, enabling rapid iteration without physical prototyping costs.

Key Parameters

The optimization focuses on tuning critical design variables that directly influence jumping performance:

Design Variables

Link lengths (A, B, C, D)
Spring stiffness coefficients
Joint damping values
Mass distribution
Actuation timing sequence
Servo angular range

Methodology

Our optimization pipeline follows a systematic 4-step approach:

STEP 01

Define Design Space

Establish parameter bounds based on manufacturing constraints and motor specifications.

STEP 02

Run Simulations

Execute MuJoCo simulations across parameter combinations using Latin Hypercube sampling.

STEP 03

Analyze Results

Extract performance metrics and identify Pareto-optimal configurations.

STEP 04

Validate & Refine

Cross-validate top candidates and perform sensitivity analysis.

Optimization Results

üéØ Key Findings

The optimization revealed that the Medium configuration (Link A=5cm, Link D=7cm) achieves the best balance between jump height and stability. This configuration maximizes the kinematic advantage while staying within the SG90 servo's torque capacity of 0.15 Nm.

Larger link geometries showed diminishing returns due to actuator limitations, while smaller configurations were constrained by reduced range of motion. The optimized design achieves a 114mm maximum jump height ‚Äî a 75% improvement over the baseline.
